The Rock and the Sea

A Dialogue of Nature

ü™® The Rock Speaks

"I am the Rock... I am set to limit thy power."
The Rock represents stability, pride, and resistance. It claims it will stop the sea, whether the sea is angry or gentle.

üåä The Sea Speaks

"I am the Sea. I hold the land."
The Sea represents time, change, and persistence. It turns granite to clay and cliffs to pebbles. It plays with the land.

‚ú® Poetic Devices

Personification Contrast (Stability vs Change) Imagery Metaphor

Moral: Nothing stays unchanged forever; persistence (water) overcomes resistance (rock) over time.

The Elephant and the Tragopan

A Plea for Conservation

üêò The Elephant's Speech

The Elephant acts as the leader. He describes humans as "mild and vicious" ‚Äî capable of creating art but also destroying nature. He warns the animals that humans will cut the bamboo, burn the rhododendrons, and build reservoirs.

Human Cruelty

Wearing leopard spots, using ivory for combs, shooting birds for fun, and forcing bears to dance.

The Warning

"When the road comes, the forest goes." Pollution (soot/filth) will kill the fish (Trout). Leopards and deer will lose their homes.

The Solution

Unity. "We must unite in fur and feather / For we will live or die together."
